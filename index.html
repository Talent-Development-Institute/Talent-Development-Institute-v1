About

Have you ever lied on your resumé or CV?

Academic and professional attributes are mostly unverified and usually untrusted, but those days are over — TDI is bringing verifiable credentials to students, workers, and employers all over the world.

Technologies

Built with privacy-preserving cryptography and blockchain

Searchable Encryption

Traditional encryption provides confidentiality, but disallows useful operations like indexing. Searchable encryption (SE) provide confidentiality and searchability simultaneously: the data owner can delegate a token for a specific query, which allows the server to perform the query over encrypted data. Thus, TDI verifiable credentials are safe against a database hack.

Zero-Knowledge Proof

A zero-knowledge proof (ZKP) is a cryptographic method which allows one person (the prover) to prove to another person (the verifier) that they have the possession of some information without revealing the information to the verifier. In other words, ZKP allows conveying the assurance that the information is in hand without revealing the information itself.

Your skills were acquired over a very long career: schooling, volunteering, extracurriculars, internships, and perhaps even, real jobs. Stand out from the posers with a TDI Skills Passport that proves exactly what you did and when you did it.

For each experience or achievement, a dependable or primary entity determines whether that experience or achievement is gained or earned. If satisfactory, that entity digitally-signs a verifiable credential.

You hold the verified credentials in their TDI Skills Passport, while the digital signatures from the verification of the relevant trusted authority are secured on a blockchain registry to be validated at any time.

Hiring the right people

They have a particular set of skills...

Were those skills acquired over a very long career? With the appropriate authorities, TDI Skills Passport lets people collect those skills in a trusted and verifiable manner.

  • Examine & Issue
  • Hold & Use
  • Verify

A worker completes a process set out by a relevant trusted authority, which attests to that this worker has a particular set of skills. This authority issues a digitally-signed credential proving that this skill has been earned.

The worker holds the verified credentials in their TDI Skills Passport, while the digital signatures from the verification of the relevant trusted authority are secured on a blockchain registry to be validated at any time.

An employer looking for people with a particular set of skills wants to know beyond a reasonable doubt that a worker does indeed have those skills. With permission, the employer may verify those skills and nothing else.
